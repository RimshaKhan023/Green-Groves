GardeningTools

Spade

A spade is used for digging and breaking up earth.
How to Use: Keep a straight back and lift with your legs, ensuring a firm grip.

Hoe

Hoes are used for weeding and soil preparation.
How to Use: Use with a proper stance to prevent back strain, and ensure the area is clear of rocks and debris.

Garden Fork

A garden fork is useful for turning soil and breaking up clumps.
How to Use:Keep your back straight and bend your knees when lifting to prevent back strain.

Pruner

Pruners are used for trimming and shaping plants.
How to Use: Keep the pruner locked when not in use, and ensure a comfortable grip to prevent hand strain.

Watering Can

Watering Can is used for watering plants.
How to Use:Use a can with a comfortable grip and manageable weight to prevent strain.

Bulb Planter

This tool is specially designed to make the correct-sized hole for planting bulbs and other plants.
How to Use: Use both hands to apply pressure evenly and avoid twisting your wrist or back.

Auger

It is used for making holes in the ground for planting.
How to Use: Wear gloves and eye protection, and ensure there are no underground utilities before drilling.

Bilhook Saw

It is used for trimming shoots and stems, cutting vines, severing roots, and sawing branches.
How to Use: Ensure a firm grip, wear protective gloves to prevent cuts, and clear the surrounding area of others before using.

Sprayer

Sprayers are used for applying pesticides, herbicides, or fertilizers.
How to Use: When spraying pesticides, ensure the nozzle is directed away from you to prevent chemical exposure.
